# Vertical Agentic-OS Layer — Legora · NickAI · Harvey · Imbue

**One-liner:** Four players proving the "agentic OS / agent-company" framing has gone *vertical* — domain-specialized agent stacks (legal, trading, software) that template the same recursion-of-tasks pattern our horizontal personal AI company aims to generalize. [synthesised]
**Axis:** Mostly **B** (AI company/workforce for one domain) with emerging **A** (foreground "OS" experience) — Legora and NickAI explicitly market an "OS"; Harvey is workforce-of-agents; Imbue is **Infra**-leaning (reasoning + agent runtime/UI).
**Category (3-meanings-of-OS):** Legora = 2 (agent experience layer, self-styled "operating system") · NickAI = 2 (agent experience layer for trading) · Harvey = 2 (agent workforce, no "OS" label) · Imbue = 3 (infra/kernel + agent UI, Sculptor).
**Stage / availability:** Legora aOS shipping (launched May 2026) · NickAI shipping (launched Mar 2026) · Harvey shipping at scale · Imbue Sculptor public beta · **As-of:** 2026-06

## What it does best · What it lacks · What we take

- **Best (across the pack):**
  - Legora: proves the literal phrase "agentic **operating system** for legal work" is a fundable, shipping category, with **full audit trails over every tool call, file access, and agent action** and human-review-before-commit. [1][3][5]
  - Harvey: proves an **agent workforce at scale** — 25,000+ custom agents/workflows in production — anchored on **citation traceability** (Shepard's integration) as its trust test. [4][9][10]
  - NickAI: proves the vertical-OS framing extends to **high-stakes financial autonomy** with a **non-custodial** trust posture (never holds funds) and **multi-LLM consensus** before any execution decision. [6][7][8]
  - Imbue: proves the **reasoning-first + per-agent sandbox isolation** infra pattern (Sculptor: each agent gets an isolated repo copy/container) and an explicit **"personal computer / user agency"** mission close to our Chairman framing. [11][12][13]
- **Lacks (vs. our full vision):**
  - All four are **single-domain** (one vertical); none is a horizontal personal AI company spanning a user's whole life. [synthesised]
  - **Org depth is shallow:** orchestrator → leaf-agent (2-tier), not a recursive Chairman → executives → teams → one-task employees hierarchy. [synthesised]
  - None states **post-quantum (PQC)** anything; trust is audit-trail + citations + non-custody, not cryptographic per-agent identity. [synthesised]
  - All are **enterprise/prosumer**, not *personal*; the human is a reviewer/operator, not a sovereign "Chairman of the Board." [synthesised]
- **We take:**
  - Legora's **audit-trail-over-every-action + review-before-commit** is the canonical receipts/reversibility pattern — maps to `councils/audit/` and our gates/receipts work. [1][5]
  - Harvey's **citation-traceability-as-trust-test** validates our verifier/anti-hallucination wedge as a *buyable differentiator*, not a nice-to-have. [4][10]
  - NickAI's **non-custodial least-privilege** (agent acts where assets already live, vendor never custodies) is a per-agent-security analogue worth adopting for any high-stakes leaf employee. [6][8]

## Deep dive

### Legora — legal agentic OS

Legora (Stockholm; founder/CEO Max Junestrand) is the clearest proof of the vertical-OS framing. It explicitly launched **"the Legora aOS™: the agentic operating system for legal work"** on **2026-05-07**. [3][1] The product **"centralizes and orchestrates agents with different capabilities to complete individual tasks for users"** — document intake, research, drafting, review — and connects to a firm's document management systems, legal-research databases, and email. [1] Org model is **orchestrator → specialized task-agents (2-tier)**, not recursive. [synthesised from 1]

Trust posture is notably strong and close to our wedge: although agents complete tasks independently, **"users can review the agentic processes before changes are made,"** and **"full audit trails give complete visibility into every tool call, file access, and agent action."** [5] Human expertise is injected via **Legal Engineers** (embedded lawyers/technologists) who configure the aOS per practice area — a "human-in-the-loop at deployment" model. [5]

Funding: **$550M Series D at $5.55B valuation, announced 2026-03-10**, led by Accel (Benchmark, Bessemer, General Catalyst, ICONIQ, Redpoint, Y Combinator participating). [2][14] Extended in April 2026 to **~$600M / $5.6B post-money** adding **Atlassian and NVentures (NVIDIA's VC arm)**. [the Series D extension press, Apr 2026][2] **Crossed $100M ARR in Q1 2026**; **1,200+ law firms / in-house teams across 50+ markets** including White & Case, Cleary Gottlieb, Linklaters, Goodwin. [1][14] Local/cloud: cloud SaaS. [synthesised]

### NickAI — agentic trading OS (confirmed)

NickAI Inc. (Delaware; HQ New York) publicly launched on **2026-03-12** as **"the first agentic operating system for autonomous financial strategies."** [6][7][8] Users build no-code AI agents that **"watch markets, reason about what they see, and act on that analysis around the clock."** [6] A **visual workflow editor** snaps together market-data feeds, **multiple LLMs**, custom logic, and execution endpoints into a continuous pipeline; rather than one model, NickAI routes inputs through **several LLMs and combines outputs (consensus) before a single execution decision.** [6][7] Markets: **crypto, equities, prediction markets**, with integrations to Hyperliquid, Coinbase, OKX, Polymarket. [6][8] Org model: **pipeline/orchestrator of model-modules → execution (2-tier, flat-ish)**; not a recursive org. [synthesised]

Trust posture centers on a **platform-agnostic, non-custodial architecture: "NickAI does not custody user funds"** — users connect existing accounts/wallets and agents operate where assets already live. [6][8] This is effectively a least-privilege/blast-radius-containment stance. Backed by **Galaxy Digital** (amount/round size **unknown**). [6][8] Pricing **unknown**. Anti-hallucination/verifier: implicit multi-LLM consensus, no dedicated verifier described. [synthesised]

### Harvey — legal AI agent workforce

Harvey (San Francisco) is the scale leader in legal AI but does **not** market an "OS"; it frames itself as **AI agents + a workforce** for law firms and enterprises. As of its March 2026 raise, **"more than 25,000 custom agents operate on Harvey,"** executing M&A, due diligence, contract drafting, and document review; customers have built **25,000+ agentic custom workflows** via Workflow Builder / Agent Builder. [4][9][10] Org model: **user-configured agents + workflows (2-tier: builder → task-agents)**, supported by embedded **legal engineering teams**. [4][10]

Trust posture is the most citation-forward of the pack: **"Citation traceability is the trust test"** — flagged clauses, extracted provisions, and redlines must trace to verifiable source material, reinforced by a **Shepard's Citations integration** to validate whether cases remain "good law." [10] This is a dedicated anti-hallucination/verifier posture aligned with our wedge.

Funding: **$200M at $11B valuation, announced 2026-03-25**, co-led by GIC and Sequoia (a16z, Coatue, Conviction, Elad Gil, Kleiner Perkins participating); total raised **~$1B**; followed a December 2025 round at $8B. [Harvey/CNBC/Bloomberg, Mar 2026][15] **~$100M ARR (as of Aug 2025)**, NRR >150% reported by third parties. [4][secondary] Cloud SaaS. [synthesised]

### Imbue — reasoning-first agents + Sculptor (infra/UI)

Imbue (San Francisco; founded 2018, rebranded from Generally Intelligent) is the **infra/kernel** end of the spectrum: it builds **reasoning-optimized foundation models** and applies them to **agents that code**, with the stated belief that **"reasoning is the primary blocker to effective AI agents."** [11][12] Mission language is the closest to ours: **"rekindle the dream of the personal computer… giving us freedom, dignity, and agency"** — a user-sovereignty / personal-computing framing. [12]

Its shipping product, **Sculptor**, is **"the missing UI for parallel coding agents"**: a desktop app where **each agent task gets an isolated copy of the repo / its own container**, enabling safe parallel execution plus a **Pairing Mode** to merge/discard agent changes. [11][13] This **per-agent sandbox isolation** is the strongest per-agent-security pattern in the pack and maps to our worktree/least-privilege model. Org depth: **parallel sibling agents (flat), human-merges** — no hierarchy. [synthesised]

Funding: **$200M Series B (Sep 2023) at >$1B valuation**, investors incl. **NVIDIA, Astera Institute, Kyle Vogt, Simon Last; +$12M from the Alexa Fund and Eric Schmidt**. [11][12] No 2026 raise surfaced (newer funding **unknown**). Sculptor is **free during beta** (needs Anthropic access via API key or Claude Pro/Max). [13][secondary] Local desktop app + cloud models = **hybrid**. [13]

## SWOT (with so-what-for-us)

- **Strengths:** The literal "agentic OS" label is now fundable across multiple verticals (Legora $5.6B, NickAI launched, Harvey $11B) — *so-what:* our "AI company / AI-OS" framing is validated, not speculative; we can template verticals later. [1][6][15]
- **Weaknesses:** All are single-domain and 2-tier; none recursive, none personal, none PQC — *so-what:* our recursive Chairman-led horizontal company and cryptographic trust layer are genuine white space. [synthesised]
- **Opportunities:** Each vertical leader (Legora/Harvey/NickAI) is a *potential template tenant or acquisition surface* for a horizontal OS that hosts vertical agent-companies — *so-what:* design our org primitives so a "legal company" or "trading company" can be instantiated as a sub-org under the Chairman. [hypothesis]
- **Threats:** Well-capitalized verticals can expand horizontally (Harvey already targets "enterprises," Legora adds DMS/email/research connectors) — *so-what:* trust (per-agent security, receipts, PQC, personal sovereignty) must be our durable moat, since incumbents can match domain depth and capital. [synthesised from 1][15]
